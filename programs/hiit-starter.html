This program assumes you can walk briskly for 20+ minutes and do basic bodyweight movements (squats, lunges, push-ups). If you're a complete beginner, complete the Cardio Foundation program first.

What is HIIT?

High-Intensity Interval Training alternates between periods of intense effort and recovery. Research shows it improves cardiovascular fitness, burns calories efficiently, and can be completed in less time than traditional cardio.

Week 1-2: Introduction

Pattern: 30 sec work / 60 sec rest √ó 8 rounds

Movements: High knees, squats, mountain climbers (slow), jumping jacks (or step jacks)

Intensity: 6-7 out of 10 effort

Total time: ~15 minutes including warmup/cooldown

Week 3-4: Building

Pattern: 30 sec work / 45 sec rest √ó 10 rounds

Movements: Add burpees (modified), squat jumps (or fast squats), plank jacks

Intensity: 7-8 out of 10 effort

Total time: ~18 minutes including warmup/cooldown

Week 5-6: Intensity

Pattern: 40 sec work / 40 sec rest √ó 12 rounds

Movements: Full burpees, tuck jumps (or high knees fast), sprint in place

Intensity: 8-9 out of 10 effort

Total time: ~22 minutes including warmup/cooldown

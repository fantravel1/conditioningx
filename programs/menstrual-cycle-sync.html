Cycle-Synced Training

4 weeks of training optimized for your menstrual cycle. Work with your hormones, not against them.

4

Weeks

4-5x

Female-Specific

Developed with Dr. Stacy Sims

This program is based on the research of Dr. Stacy Sims, author of ROAR. It recognizes that women's hormones create a natural training periodization—we just need to work with it.

The Science

Your menstrual cycle creates a hormonal environment that affects energy, recovery, strength, and endurance. By matching training intensity to your cycle phase, you optimize adaptation while minimizing burnout.

Week 1: Menstruation (Days 1-7)

Hormones: Estrogen and progesterone at lowest levels

How you may feel: Low energy, possible cramping, fatigue

Training focus: Listen to your body. Light movement often helps.

Workout Schedule

  • Day 1-2: Rest or gentle walking (15-20 min)
  • Day 3: Light yoga or mobility (20 min)
  • Day 4: Easy Zone 2 cardio (25 min)
  • Day 5: Bodyweight circuit - low intensity (20 min)
  • Day 6: Rest or gentle walk
  • Day 7: Zone 2 cardio (30 min)

Key Points

  • No pressure to perform
  • Movement often reduces cramps
  • Skip if you feel terrible—no guilt
  • Iron-rich foods if bleeding is heavy

Week 2: Follicular Phase (Days 8-14)

Hormones: Estrogen rising steadily

How you may feel: Increasing energy, positive mood, ready to push

Training focus: This is your POWER week. Go hard!

Workout Schedule

  • Day 8: HIIT session (25 min)
  • Day 9: Strength + cardio circuit (35 min)
  • Day 10: Active recovery - easy cardio (20 min)
  • Day 11: High-intensity intervals (30 min)
  • Day 12: Full body strength + cardio (40 min)
  • Day 13: Zone 2 endurance (45 min)
  • Day 14: HIIT or sprint work (25 min)

Key Points

  • Schedule your hardest workouts here
  • PRs are more likely this week
  • Estrogen supports muscle building
  • Pain tolerance is higher
  • Take advantage of the energy!

Week 3: Ovulation → Early Luteal (Days 15-21)

Hormones: Estrogen peaks then drops, progesterone rising

How you may feel: Energy starts high then may dip mid-week

Training focus: Transition week. Start strong, taper toward end.

Workout Schedule

  • Day 15: Moderate intensity cardio (35 min)
  • Day 16: Strength circuit (30 min)
  • Day 17: HIIT - if feeling good (25 min)
  • Day 18: Active recovery (20 min)
  • Day 19: Zone 2-3 cardio (35 min)
  • Day 20: Light strength + mobility (30 min)
  • Day 21: Easy Zone 2 (30 min)

Key Points

  • ACL injury risk may be slightly higher
  • Focus on controlled movements
  • Body temp rising—stay hydrated
  • Energy may fluctuate

Week 4: Late Luteal / PMS (Days 22-28)

Hormones: Both hormones dropping toward menstruation

How you may feel: PMS symptoms possible—bloating, mood changes, fatigue

Training focus: Steady-state cardio. Be patient with yourself.

Workout Schedule

  • Day 22: Zone 2 cardio (35 min)
  • Day 23: Light bodyweight + mobility (25 min)
  • Day 24: Easy Zone 2 (30 min)
  • Day 25: Yoga or stretching (30 min)
  • Day 26: Zone 2 cardio (30 min)
  • Day 27: Light movement or rest
  • Day 28: Gentle activity or rest

Key Points

  • Endurance feels harder—it's hormonal
  • Cravings are normal
  • Zone 2 is your friend this week
  • Don't force high intensity
  • Self-compassion is key

Tracking Your Cycle

To use this program effectively, you need to know where you are in your cycle. Use a period tracking app or calendar. Note: If you're on hormonal birth control, your cycle is suppressed—this program may not apply the same way.

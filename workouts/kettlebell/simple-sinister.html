The Pavel Tsatsouline Standard

Simple & Sinister is Pavel's answer to "what's the minimum effective dose for strength and conditioning?" Two movements. Done daily. For life. The swing builds explosive hip power and cardiovascular conditioning. The get-up builds total-body strength and stability. That's it. That's the program.

The Workout

Warm Up (5 minutes)

3 rounds of:

  • 5 Goblet squats (pause at bottom)
  • 5 Hip bridges
  • 5 Halos each direction

Light kettlebell. Grease the groove.

Part 1: Swings

100 One-Arm Swings

  • 10 sets of 10 reps
  • Switch hands every set (10 left, 10 right, etc.)
  • Rest as needed between sets (aim for 30-60 sec)
  • Goal: Complete in ~10 minutes

Standard: Complete all 100 swings in 5 minutes or less to achieve "Simple"

Part 2: Turkish Get-Ups

10 Turkish Get-Ups (5 each side)

  • 1 rep at a time
  • Alternate sides: left, right, left, right...
  • Full get-up: lying → standing → lying
  • Rest as needed between reps

Standard: Complete all 10 get-ups in 10 minutes or less to achieve "Simple"

Kettlebell Weight Standards

Women

  • Simple: 24kg (53 lbs)
  • Sinister: 32kg (70 lbs)
  • Starting: 12-16kg

Men

  • Simple: 32kg (70 lbs)
  • Sinister: 48kg (106 lbs)
  • Starting: 16-24kg

Movement Standards

One-Arm Swing

  • Hip hinge, not squat
  • Explosive hip snap
  • Arm is just a hook
  • Bell to eye level
  • Tight glutes at top
  • Controlled back swing

Turkish Get-Up

  • Arm locked out entire time
  • Eyes on kettlebell
  • Smooth, controlled movement
  • No rushing
  • Pause at each position
  • Same path up and down

Why This Works

Swings

Build explosive hip power, posterior chain strength, and cardiovascular conditioning. 100 reps creates massive metabolic demand.

Get-Ups

Develop total-body strength, shoulder stability, and core control. Each rep is a slow, controlled full-body integration.

Programming Notes

Do This Daily

  • 5-6 days per week is ideal
  • Can be done as standalone or added to strength training
  • When times improve, increase weight
  • Never sacrifice form for speed
  • This is a practice, not a workout

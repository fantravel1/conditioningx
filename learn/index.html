Frequently Asked Questions

What is cardiovascular conditioning?

Cardiovascular conditioning is the process of improving your heart and lung efficiency through regular aerobic exercise. When you condition your cardiovascular system, your heart becomes stronger, pumping more blood with each beat, while your lungs become more efficient at oxygen exchange. Benefits include lower resting heart rate, improved endurance, better mood, and reduced risk of heart disease.

How long should a cardio workout be?

Cardio workouts can range from 5 minutes to 90+ minutes depending on your goals and intensity. For beginners, 15-20 minute sessions are a great starting point. The key is consistency ‚Äî shorter, regular workouts are more beneficial than occasional long sessions. As you build fitness, you can increase duration or intensity based on your goals.

Can you do cardio every day?

Yes, you can do cardio every day if you vary the intensity. The key is avoiding high-intensity work every day. Mix hard days with easy days (Zone 1-2 training). Beginners should start with 3-4 days per week and build up. Listen to your body ‚Äî fatigue, decreased performance, or persistent soreness are signs you need more recovery.

What is Zone 2 training and why does it matter?

Zone 2 training is low-intensity exercise at 60-70% of your max heart rate where you can easily hold a conversation. It builds your aerobic base, improves fat oxidation, and increases mitochondrial density. Elite endurance athletes spend 80% of their training in Zone 2. It may feel "too easy" but it's the foundation of cardiovascular fitness.

Is HIIT better than steady-state cardio?

Neither is inherently "better" ‚Äî they train different systems. HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) improves anaerobic capacity and is time-efficient. Steady-state cardio builds aerobic base and recovery capacity. A well-rounded program includes both. Beginners should establish a base of steady-state before adding HIIT.

Half Marathon Training

16 weeks to 13.1 miles. Your first half or a new PR.

16

Weeks

4-5x

Per Week

30-90

Min/Session

13.1

Mile Goal

Prerequisites

  • Can run 5-6 miles comfortably
  • Running consistently 3-4 times per week for at least 3 months
  • No current injuries that affect running
  • Completed a 10K or equivalent training

Program Structure

This 16-week plan builds mileage gradually, peaks around week 13, then tapers for race day. You'll run 4-5 days per week with one long run, tempo work, easy runs, and optional cross-training.

Weekly Pattern

Easy Days

3-4 runs at conversational pace

Quality Day

1 tempo or interval session

Long Run

Progressive build to 12 miles

Phase 1: Base Building (Weeks 1-4)

Goal: Establish consistent running and build to 25-30 miles per week.

Sample Week 1

  • Mon: 3 miles easy
  • Tue: Rest or cross-train
  • Wed: 4 miles easy
  • Thu: 3 miles easy
  • Fri: Rest
  • Sat: 6 miles long run
  • Sun: Rest or easy walk

Weekly: ~16 miles

Sample Week 4

  • Mon: 4 miles easy
  • Tue: Rest or cross-train
  • Wed: 5 miles with strides
  • Thu: 4 miles easy
  • Fri: Rest
  • Sat: 8 miles long run
  • Sun: Rest

Weekly: ~21 miles

Phase 2: Building (Weeks 5-8)

Goal: Introduce tempo runs, build to 30-35 miles per week.

Sample Week 5

  • Mon: 4 miles easy
  • Tue: Rest
  • Wed: 5 miles with 2 mile tempo
  • Thu: 4 miles easy
  • Fri: Rest or cross-train
  • Sat: 9 miles long run
  • Sun: 3 miles recovery

Weekly: ~25 miles

Sample Week 8

  • Mon: 5 miles easy
  • Tue: Rest
  • Wed: 6 miles with 3 mile tempo
  • Thu: 5 miles easy
  • Fri: Rest
  • Sat: 10 miles long run
  • Sun: 4 miles recovery

Weekly: ~30 miles

Phase 3: Peak (Weeks 9-13)

Goal: Highest mileage weeks, longest long runs, sharpening workouts.

Sample Week 11

  • Mon: 5 miles easy
  • Tue: 5 miles with 4 mile tempo
  • Wed: Rest or cross-train
  • Thu: 6 miles easy
  • Fri: Rest
  • Sat: 12 miles long run
  • Sun: 4 miles recovery

Weekly: ~32 miles (peak)

Week 13 (Peak Long Run)

  • Mon: 4 miles easy
  • Tue: 6 miles with race pace miles
  • Wed: Rest
  • Thu: 5 miles easy
  • Fri: Rest
  • Sat: 12 miles (last long run)
  • Sun: 3 miles easy

Final peak week before taper

Phase 4: Taper (Weeks 14-16)

Goal: Reduce volume while maintaining sharpness. Arrive at race day fresh.

Week 14-15

  • Reduce weekly mileage by 25-30%
  • Maintain easy run frequency
  • One short tempo or race pace run
  • Long run drops to 8-10 miles

Week 16 (Race Week)

Key Workouts Explained

Tempo Runs

"Comfortably hard" paceâ€”about 25-30 sec per mile slower than 5K race pace. Should be sustainable but challenging. Builds lactate threshold.

Long Runs

Slow and steady, 1-2 min per mile slower than goal race pace. These build endurance and mental toughness. Don't chase pace.

Easy Runs

Truly easyâ€”you should be able to hold a conversation. These build aerobic base and allow recovery. Most of your running should be easy.

Race Pace Miles

Practice your goal pace in short segments. Helps calibrate effort and build confidence. Include in long runs during peak phase.

Race Day Strategy

Execute Your Best Race

  • Miles 1-3: Start conservatively. Adrenaline will make pace feel easy. Trust the plan.
  • Miles 4-8: Settle into rhythm. Stay mentally present. Take fluids at aid stations.
  • Miles 9-11: The challenge begins. Stay strong mentally. One mile at a time.
  • Miles 12-13.1: Empty the tank. You've trained for this. Sprint if you have it!

Cardiovascular Basics

Understand how your heart works, why cardio matters, and how to train it effectively.

Your Heart: The Ultimate Muscle

Your heart beats about 100,000 times per day, pumping 2,000 gallons of blood. It's the most important muscle in your body, and like any muscle, it responds to training.

Key Concept: Cardiac Output

How Your Cardiovascular System Adapts

When you train consistently, remarkable adaptations occur:

Short-Term (Weeks 1-4)

  • Increased blood plasma volume - Your blood literally increases in volume
  • Improved muscle blood flow - Better delivery of oxygen and nutrients
  • Enhanced nerve recruitment - Your body learns the movements

Medium-Term (Months 1-3)

  • Left ventricle enlargement - Your heart's main pumping chamber gets bigger
  • Increased capillary density - More blood vessels to working muscles
  • Mitochondrial biogenesis - More cellular energy factories

Long-Term (6+ Months)

  • Significantly lower resting heart rate - Athletes often have RHR of 40-50 bpm
  • Higher VO2 max - Your maximum oxygen processing capacity increases
  • Improved fat oxidation - Better at using fat for fuel

The Minimum Effective Dose

You don't need hours of cardio. Research shows significant benefits from:

150

75

or

Mix

That's just 22 minutes of moderate cardio per day, or 11 minutes of vigorous cardio. Totally achievable.

Getting Started

If you're new to cardio, start simple:

  1. Walk more. It counts. Daily walks are powerful.
  2. Find something you enjoy. The best cardio is the one you'll actually do.
  3. Start with 10 minutes. Build from there.
  4. Consistency beats intensity. Regular easy sessions trump occasional hard ones.

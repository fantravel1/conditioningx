Faster 5K Program

6 weeks of targeted speedwork to set a new personal record.

6

Weeks

4-5x

Per Week

25-50

Min/Session

PR

Goal

Prerequisites

  • Can comfortably run 5K
  • Running consistently for at least 2-3 months
  • Current 5K time established (you need a baseline)
  • Ready for harder training

The Speed Formula

Getting faster at 5K requires three elements: improved aerobic capacity, faster leg turnover, and race-specific fitness. This program addresses all three through structured intervals, tempo runs, and strides.

Weekly Structure

Day 1

Interval workout

Day 2

Easy run + strides

Day 3

Tempo run

Day 4

Week 1

  • Day 1 - Intervals: 10 min easy, 6x400m at goal 5K pace (90 sec rest), 10 min easy
  • Day 2 - Easy + Strides: 25 min easy, then 4x100m strides
  • Day 3 - Tempo: 10 min easy, 15 min tempo (comfortably hard), 10 min easy
  • Day 4 - Long: 40 min easy
  • Day 5: Rest or cross-train

Week 2

  • Day 1 - Intervals: 10 min easy, 5x600m at goal 5K pace (2 min rest), 10 min easy
  • Day 2 - Easy + Strides: 30 min easy, then 5x100m strides
  • Day 3 - Tempo: 10 min easy, 18 min tempo, 10 min easy
  • Day 4 - Long: 45 min easy
  • Day 5: Rest or cross-train

Week 3

  • Day 1 - Intervals: 10 min easy, 4x800m at goal 5K pace (2.5 min rest), 10 min easy
  • Day 2 - Easy + Strides: 30 min easy, then 6x100m strides
  • Day 3 - Tempo: 10 min easy, 20 min tempo, 10 min easy
  • Day 4 - Long: 50 min easy
  • Day 5: Rest or cross-train

Week 4

  • Day 1 - Intervals: 10 min easy, 3x1000m at goal 5K pace (3 min rest), 10 min easy
  • Day 2 - Easy + Strides: 25 min easy, then 4x100m strides
  • Day 3 - Tempo: 10 min easy, 15 min tempo, 10 min easy
  • Day 4 - Long: 40 min easy
  • Day 5: Rest

Recovery week - slightly reduced volume

Week 5

  • Day 1 - Intervals: 10 min easy, 6x600m at slightly faster than goal pace (2 min rest), 10 min easy

  • Day 4 - Long: 45 min easy
  • Day 5: Rest or cross-train

Understanding the Workouts

Strides

100m accelerations to ~90% effort. Focus on smooth, relaxed speed. Full recovery between. Builds leg speed without fatigue.

Goal Pace

Calculate from your target 5K time. If you want to run 25:00, your goal pace is 8:03/mile or ~2:00/400m.

Tempo Runs

About 15-20 sec slower per mile than goal 5K pace. Builds lactate threshold‚Äîthe pace you can sustain for an hour.

Easy Runs

Critical for recovery. 60-90 sec slower than goal pace. If they feel too slow, you're doing it right.

Race Day Strategy

Execute Your PR

  • Mile 1: Start controlled. Adrenaline will make goal pace feel easy. Trust the watch.
  • Mile 2: Settle in. This is the hardest mile mentally. Stay focused on form.
  • Mile 3: Now you race. If you have anything left, use it. Empty the tank.
  • Final 0.1: Sprint to the line. Leave nothing behind.

Female Training / Menstrual Cycle

Training with Your Menstrual Cycle

Your hormones create a natural training periodization. Learn to work with your body, not against it.

The Science

Research from Dr. Stacy Sims and others shows that women can optimize training by aligning workouts with their hormonal cycle. This isn't about training less—it's about training smarter.

Understanding Your Cycle

A typical menstrual cycle is 21-35 days. Hormonal fluctuations throughout the cycle affect energy, strength, recovery, and even injury risk.

Phase-by-Phase Training Guide

Phase 1: Early Follicular (Days 1-5)

What's happening: Menstruation. Estrogen and progesterone at lowest levels.

Training Recommendations

  • Intensity: Listen to your body. Some women feel fine, others need lighter days.
  • Good options: Light cardio, walking, yoga, gentle movement
  • If you feel good: Train normally—there's no reason to skip training
  • Recovery: May be slightly impaired; prioritize sleep

Tip: Light exercise often reduces cramps better than rest.

Phase 2: Late Follicular (Days 6-14)

What's happening: Estrogen rises steadily. Peak time for training adaptations.

Training Recommendations

  • Intensity: HIGH. This is your power phase.
  • Good options: HIIT, heavy strength, speed work, challenging cardio
  • Why it works: Estrogen supports muscle building and power output
  • Recovery: Enhanced—your body adapts well to hard training

Tip: Schedule your hardest workouts and PRs here.

Phase 3: Ovulation (Around Day 14)

What's happening: Estrogen peaks, testosterone briefly spikes.

Training Recommendations

  • Intensity: Many women feel strongest here
  • Good options: Strength work, power movements, explosive training
  • Caution: ACL injury risk may be elevated due to ligament laxity
  • Focus on: Controlled movements, proper form, avoid extreme plyometrics

Tip: Great time for testing 1-rep maxes or race-pace efforts.

Phase 4: Luteal (Days 15-28)

What's happening: Progesterone rises, core temperature increases, PMS possible.

Training Recommendations

  • Intensity: Moderate. Shift focus slightly.
  • Good options: Steady-state cardio, moderate strength, Zone 2 work
  • Challenges: Higher body temp, may tire more easily, mood changes
  • Recovery: May take longer—don't stack hard sessions

Tip: Increase sodium and hydration to account for fluid shifts.

Practical Implementation

Start Simple

  1. Track your cycle for 2-3 months to understand your patterns
  2. Note how you feel on different days (energy, strength, mood)
  3. Adjust gradually—don't overhaul your entire program at once
  4. Be flexible—not every cycle is the same
  5. If on hormonal birth control: You may not have these fluctuations; experiment with what works for you

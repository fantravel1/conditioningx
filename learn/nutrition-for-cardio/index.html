Nutrition for Cardiovascular Training

Fuel your workouts, optimize recovery, and perform your best. The practical guide to eating for cardio.

The Bottom Line

Nutrition for cardio doesn't need to be complicated. Focus on: adequate fuel before training, hydration during, and protein + carbs after. Everything else is optimization.

Pre-Workout Nutrition

What you eat before training affects energy, performance, and how you feel during your workout.

Timing Guidelines

2-3 hours before: Full meal with carbs, protein, moderate fat

1-2 hours before: Lighter meal or large snack, lower fat/fiber

30-60 minutes before: Small snack, easy to digest carbs

Fasted training: Works for low-moderate intensity. Not ideal for high-intensity or long sessions.

Pre-Workout Meal Ideas

2-3 Hours Before

  • Oatmeal with banana and peanut butter
  • Rice with chicken and vegetables
  • Whole grain toast with eggs
  • Pasta with lean meat sauce

30-60 Minutes Before

  • Banana
  • Rice cakes with honey
  • Sports drink
  • Small handful of dried fruit

During Training

For workouts under 60-90 minutes, water is usually enough. Longer sessions may benefit from additional fuel.

Hydration

Before: 16-20 oz water 2-3 hours before, 8 oz 20-30 minutes before

During: 4-8 oz every 15-20 minutes (more in heat)

Signs of dehydration: Dark urine, headache, fatigue, decreased performance

Fueling Longer Sessions (90+ minutes)

  • 30-60g carbs per hour for intense exercise
  • Sports drinks, gels, chews, or real food (dates, banana)
  • Practice during training‚Äîdon't try new fuel on race day

Post-Workout Recovery

Recovery nutrition helps replenish glycogen, repair muscle, and prepare for your next session.

The Recovery Window

The "30-minute window" is less critical than once thought, but eating within 2 hours of training is still beneficial.

Target: 20-40g protein + carbs (roughly 3:1 or 4:1 carb to protein ratio)

Examples: Chocolate milk, protein shake with banana, Greek yogurt with fruit, chicken with rice

Common Mistakes

  • Under-eating: Chronic calorie restriction hurts performance and recovery
  • Low carb for cardio: You need glycogen for intense work
  • Skipping post-workout nutrition: Delays recovery, especially with frequent training
  • Over-reliance on supplements: Food first, supplements second
  • Not practicing race-day nutrition: Train your gut like you train your body

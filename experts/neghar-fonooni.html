Philosophy

Neghar Fonooni is a fitness writer, coach, and advocate for radically self-loving fitness. Her approach centers on finding joy in movement rather than exercising as punishment. She emphasizes that fitness should enhance your life, not consume it.

Core Principles

  • Joyful Movement - Exercise should feel good, not punitive
  • Intuitive Fitness - Listen to your body's needs each day
  • Self-Compassion - Speak to yourself like a friend
  • Flexibility Over Rigidity - Adapt your routine to your life
  • Progress, Not Perfection - Celebrate every step forward

Key Teachings for Cardio

1. Choose Activities You Love

The best cardio is the one you'll actually do. If you hate running, don't run. Dance, hike, swim, play—find your thing and do it often.

2. Reject the "Burn" Mentality

You don't need to "earn" your food or "burn off" last night's dinner. Movement is not punishment. It's a celebration of what your body can do.

3. Honor Rest

Rest days are not lazy days. They're growth days. Your body adapts during recovery. Pushing through fatigue is not discipline—it's often self-sabotage.

4. Move for Mental Health

Some of the best workouts aren't about physical gains. A walk when you're stressed, dancing when you're sad, yoga when you're anxious—these are valid uses of movement.

Applying Her Principles

Before Your Workout

Ask yourself: "What does my body need today?" Not what does your program say, not what you "should" do—what do you genuinely need? Some days that's a hard workout. Some days it's a gentle walk.

During Your Workout

Notice how you feel. Is this bringing you energy or draining you? Are you present in your body or dissociating? Adjust intensity based on real-time feedback.

Who Benefits from Her Approach

  • People recovering from disordered exercise patterns
  • Those who've burned out on rigid programs
  • Anyone who dreads their workouts
  • People seeking sustainable, lifelong fitness
  • Those wanting to improve their relationship with their body

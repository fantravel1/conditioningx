Calculate your personalized heart rate training zones for optimal cardiovascular conditioning.

Used to estimate your maximum heart rate

How to Measure Resting Heart Rate

  1. Measure first thing in the morning, before getting out of bed
  2. Use a heart rate monitor, fitness watch, or find your pulse
  3. Count beats for 60 seconds (or 15 seconds x 4)
  4. Average 3-5 days for best accuracy

Typical resting HR: 60-80 bpm (lower is generally better fitness)

Understanding Heart Rate Zones

Zone 1: Recovery

Very light effort. Full conversation possible. Active recovery, warm-up.

Zone 2: Aerobic Base

Light effort. Easy conversation. Builds aerobic foundation. 80% of training here.

Zone 3: Tempo

Moderate effort. Sentences only. Improves aerobic capacity.

Zone 4: Threshold

Hard effort. Short phrases only. Increases lactate threshold.

Zone 5: Maximum

Maximum effort. Can't talk. Sprint intervals, VO2 max work.

The Science Behind the Calculator

Maximum Heart Rate Formula

We use the Tanaka formula (2001), which is more accurate than the traditional 220-age:

Max HR = 208 - (0.7 × age)

This formula was validated in a meta-analysis of 351 studies with 18,712 subjects.

Heart Rate Reserve Method

When you provide resting HR, we use the Karvonen method for more personalized zones:

Target HR = ((Max HR - Resting HR) × %intensity) + Resting HR

This accounts for your individual fitness level through heart rate reserve (HRR).

Why Swimming for Cardio?

Swimming is the ultimate low-impact, full-body cardio. No pounding on joints, natural resistance in all directions, and the cooling effect of water lets you work harder longer. It's perfect for active recovery, cross-training, or as your primary cardio modality.

Pool Workouts by Goal

Endurance Building

30 min

Steady State Swim

Zone 2Aerobic
  • Warm up: 200m easy
  • Main: 1500-2000m at steady, comfortable pace
  • Cool down: 200m easy
  • Focus: Consistent stroke, breathing rhythm
35 min

Pyramid Build

ProgressiveMental focus
  • 100m, 200m, 300m, 400m, 300m, 200m, 100m
  • 20 sec rest between each
  • Descend pace (get faster) on way up
  • Total: 1600m + warm up/cool down

Interval Training

25 min

50s on the Minute

Speed workLactate
  • Warm up: 400m easy
  • 12-16 x 50m on 1:00 (rest is whatever's left)
  • Goal: consistent times
  • Cool down: 200m easy
30 min

100s Threshold Set

Race paceChallenging
  • Warm up: 400m with drills
  • 8 x 100m at threshold pace
  • 15-20 sec rest between
  • Cool down: 300m easy

Technique + Conditioning

Drill-Swim Combo (2000m total)

  • Warm up: 200m easy free
  • Drill set: 4 x 50m (25 drill / 25 swim) - focus: catch
  • Main set: 8 x 100m at moderate effort, 15 sec rest
  • Kick set: 4 x 50m kick only, 20 sec rest
  • Pull set: 4 x 100m with pull buoy, focus on rotation
  • Cool down: 200m easy backstroke

Good technique IS good conditioning. Inefficient strokes waste energy.

Dryland Training for Swimmers

What you do out of the pool matters too.

Core Circuit

  • Plank: 45 sec
  • Flutter kicks: 30 sec
  • Russian twists: 20 each
  • Superman holds: 30 sec
  • 3 rounds, minimal rest

Shoulder Stability

  • Band pull-aparts: 15
  • External rotations: 12 each
  • Y-T-W raises: 10 each
  • Swimmer's prayer stretch
  • 2-3 rounds, injury prevention

Jump Rope Cardio

  • 3 min on, 1 min off
  • 5 rounds total
  • Builds ankle/calf for starts
  • Great on non-pool days

For Non-Swimmers

Getting Started in the Pool

  • Can't swim laps? Start with water walking, treading water, or aqua jogging
  • Out of breath quickly? Use fins to help you move and focus on breathing
  • Technique issues? Take a few lessons‚Äîit transforms the experience
  • Pool anxiety? Start in the shallow end, build confidence gradually

Cross-Training Benefits

For Runners

Swimming gives your joints a break while maintaining cardio. Perfect for recovery days or when injured. Aqua jogging mimics running without impact.

For Lifters

Low-impact conditioning that won't interfere with leg day. Easy swimming between sets of hard training. Shoulder health benefits for pressing.

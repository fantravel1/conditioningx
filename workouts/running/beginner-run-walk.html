Before You Start

  • Wear comfortable running shoes
  • Choose a flat, safe route (sidewalk, track, or treadmill)
  • Bring water if it's hot
  • Set a timer or use a running app

The Workout

Warm Up (5 minutes)

Walk at a comfortable pace. Start slow and gradually increase to a brisk walk. Swing your arms naturally. This prepares your body for running.

Running Tips for Beginners

Pace Yourself

If you can't hold a conversation while running, you're going too fast. Slow down. Speed comes later.

Walk Breaks Are Strategy

Walk breaks aren't cheating‚Äîthey're smart. Even experienced runners use them in races. No shame in walking.

Breathe Naturally

Don't overthink breathing. In through nose, out through mouth, or whatever feels natural. Your body knows what to do.

Land Lightly

Don't stomp. Land mid-foot, with feet under your body, not out in front. Short, quick steps are better than long strides.

Progression

Do this workout 3 times over a week or two. When it feels comfortable, progress:

  • Week 1-2: 2 min run / 1 min walk (this workout)
  • Week 3-4: 3 min run / 1 min walk
  • Week 5-6: 4 min run / 1 min walk
  • Week 7-8: 5 min run / 1 min walk
  • Week 9+: 10 min run / 1 min walk ‚Üí continuous running

Philosophy

Nia Shanks promotes a "sane and simple" approach to fitness. Her core message: strength training makes everything better, and you don't need complicated programs or hours in the gym. Focus on getting stronger at a few key movements, eat well, and live your life.

The Lift Like a Girl Principles

  • Strength First - Build strength; everything else follows
  • Minimum Effective Dose - Do enough to progress, not more
  • Focus on Performance - Chase PRs, not calorie burns
  • Simple Over Complex - Basic movements, done well
  • Sustainable Always - If you can't maintain it, don't do it

Cardio in the Nia Shanks Approach

1. Cardio Supports, Doesn't Dominate

Cardio is valuable for health and recovery, but it shouldn't be the centerpiece of your program. A few sessions per week—done in ways you enjoy—is plenty for most people.

2. Strength Training IS Cardio

Circuit-style strength training with minimal rest provides significant cardiovascular benefits. You're building muscle AND conditioning simultaneously.

3. Walking Is Underrated

Daily walking is the most sustainable, lowest-stress form of cardio. 20-30 minutes most days does more for long-term health than sporadic intense sessions.

4. Don't Outrun Bad Nutrition

No amount of cardio fixes poor eating habits. Get nutrition right first. Then cardio serves its true purpose: improving cardiovascular health and endurance.

Sample Weekly Structure

Strength (3x/week)

  • Squat variation
  • Hinge (deadlift) variation
  • Push (press or pushup)
  • Pull (row or pullup)

30-45 min sessions, progressive overload

Cardio (2-3x/week)

  • Daily walking (20-30 min)
  • 1-2 higher intensity sessions
  • Activity you enjoy

Supports recovery, doesn't interfere with strength

Key Takeaways

What to Embrace

  • Strength training as foundation
  • Simple, repeatable programs
  • Performance-based goals
  • Daily movement (walking)
  • Long-term thinking

What to Avoid

  • Excessive cardio for fat loss
  • Complicated workout programs
  • Scale-obsessed thinking
  • Punishment-based exercise
  • Unsustainable extremes
